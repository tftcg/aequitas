Wheeljack’s Warehouse: INVERTICONS!

Follow

Hello!

This is Scott Van Essen from the Transformers TCG design team, and I’m very pleased to introduce a new periodic feature here at Transformers TCG central: Wheeljack’s Warehouse!

In this series, we’ll be sharing decks that we think you’ll find interesting, and are totally, definitely, not at all dangerous. Now, while we may occasionally highlight a deck that won an event, we will not be focusing on the most powerful decks. Rather, we will primarily be featuring under-the-radar decks, some of our personal favorites, build-arounds based on interesting interactions, and some top-notch grade-A-certified R&D-flavored jank; all in the hope that we spur all of you to explore new ideas and maybe surprise your playgroup with something never-before seen. We encourage you to view these decklists as the beginning (not the end) of your experimentation.

For our debut, I’m sharing a deck I’ve been noodling on for a few weeks, Inverted Constructicons, or “Inverticons” to pair with the Constructicons, a.k.a. the six characters found in the Devastator Deck.

Actions
3 Heavy Landing
3 Work overtime
2 Constructicon Enigma
3 Security Checkpoint
3 The Bigger They Are
3 Reclaim
2 Vaporize
1 Battlefield Report
1 Take Cover
1 Dampening Field

Upgrades
2 Inverted
3 Handheld Blaster
1 Scoundrel’s Blaster
3 Erratic Energy Grenade
1 Thermal Weaponry
2 Laser Cutlass
1 Bashing Shield
3 Data Bank
1 Enforcement Batons
1 Smoke Cloak

The deck starts out as a heavily defensive Constructicon build, trying to minimize the damage it takes while poking through for a bit with pierce. Once you combine, you want to get Inverted! on Devastator to take advantage of his bold and start knocking your opponent’s team into the bleachers. If everything works out right, you’ll have saved enough damage early that you can soak up a number of hits while dealing massive damage swings and quickly knocking your opponent’s team out of action.

The deck runs Work Overtime and Data Bank to keep your hand full while building up your tower. There’s a toolbox of upgrades that you can access with Reclaim and Green battle icons. The Bigger They Are… and Heavy Landing can push through large amounts of damage before you combine. There’s a triplet of singleton secret actions to keep your opponent on your toes, and Erratic Energy Grenade because your team is going to get KO’d quickly.

My favorite interaction of this deck is that if your opponent wants to scrap your Inverted!, then they end up making you stronger on defense. Hopefully you’ve got the other one or a Reclaim in your hand so you can put it right back on and make them hurt on the swingback.

I hope you take a look at this deck, it’s a ton of fun and can really surprise your friends across the table with just how much damage it does.
